Section 112 of Income Tax Act – Long Term Capital Gains Explained

Section 112 of the Income Tax Act deals with taxation of long-term capital gains. Capital gains arise when you sell an asset at a profit. The tax treatment depends on the type of asset and the holding period.

What is a Long-Term Capital Asset?

An asset becomes long-term when it is held for more than the prescribed period. For listed equity shares, it is usually more than 12 months. For other assets, it may be 24 or 36 months.

Tax Rate Under Section 112

Long-term capital gains are generally taxed at 20% with indexation benefit. In some cases, 10% without indexation may apply.

Indexation Benefit

Indexation adjusts the purchase cost of the asset according to inflation. This reduces taxable gain and lowers your tax burden.

Example

If you purchased property for ₹10,00,000 and after indexation the cost becomes ₹13,00,000, and you sell for ₹18,00,000, your taxable gain becomes ₹5,00,000 instead of ₹8,00,000.

Important Points

  • Applies only to long-term assets
  • Different rules for equity and debt instruments
  • Indexation may significantly reduce tax

Understanding Section 112 helps investors plan exits smartly and legally minimize tax.
